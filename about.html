Meet AnnMarie!

The Business Mentor for ADHD Entrepreneurs

Welcome!

I am a HUGE Life Enthusiast who is on a mission to help ADHD entrepreneurs move out of their own way step into their magic so they can create the business of their dreams!

Through my free content and my coaching programs – I actively create change in this world to shift that narrative and to give people with ADHD the right tools to navigate their lives and business.

Read my story to learn how I changed my life around and inturn have helped countless clients transform their lives and their businesses!

Hi, I'm AnnMarie

Around the age of 12, I was diagnosed with ADHD and Dyslexia

I remember the day vividly. I was in the psychologists office. I didn’t want to be there. I was annoyed and upset. I had to make up an excuse to my friends and told them I was sick when they asked where I was.

Reading the test results made me angry. They were the most sterile review about who I was as a person. They were written as if I was never going to read them but told me everything I was horrible at. I hated every minute of it.

At the time, I viewed ADHD as a cruse. I was placed into separate classes for math and English. A friend of mine who was in the classes with me – we called them the “stupid math and English classes” because we had a 1 or 2 other students with us and one teacher.

I received extra time on tests and extra tutoring. I would still pretend I was no different than the other kids, even though they would ask “why did it take you so long to write the test?”, “where did you go?”, “why do you get extra time?”, “what makes you so special that you get extensions on assignments?”

Looking back I am so grateful for this experience

My mom was such an advocate in getting extra support for me through school. I have an entire life time of experience navigating the system with ADHD. Over the years, I grew resentful towards my brain. I felt as if it was letting me down. I managed to get through junior high and high school with below average grades in most classes and a lot of misconduct along the way.

I went on to complete my Diploma of Architectural Technologies in Calgary, Alberta. Prior to starting classes the College required me to get re-tested by a Psychologist to prove I still had ADHD. They wanted to confirm before they would provide extra support. The results came back – I still had ADHD and I successfully completed the program.

My grades went through the roof and I was determined to complete this course within the allotted time alongside my peers. With a new found passion for design - I wanted to get into Interior Design at university which required me to retake my English exam because my marks from high school were too low. I STRUGGLED with writing – it often brought me to tears. I wrote the exam twice and got no better results. So, my dreams of being an interior designer were put on the back burner.

Feeling a little deflated I shifted my focus

I decided to chase and endless summer and travel to Australia. I ended up travelling there for a year and I wanted to stay. So I had to sign up for university to get a visa to stay for longer. I ended up getting accepted to the beautiful Universtiy of the Sunshine Coast in Queensland for a Bachelor of Health Promotion. (They had Kangaroos on campus!) I was on medication for my ADHD at the time – and to get approved my medication – I had to go visit a family doctor who referred me to a psychologist who referred me to a Psychiatrist to ensure that I still had ADHD. Yep, it was confirmed – I still had ADHD.

Early in my studies, writing came back to haunt me. I was brand new to the Sunshine Coast and I had no friends except a boyfriend that worked away.

I went into my first week of classes – I had to take an Australia history class which I knew nothing about. They told us we had to write a paper that was due in 4 weeks. I went into a panic because I didn’t know how to write nor did I know anything about the topic.

I went home that evening and felt an impending doom. What was I thinking? Why did I sign up to this program? I promised myself when I registered I would see it through. I had moved all the way overseas away from everything I knew.

I felt alone and like my world was coming crashing down. I started to ugly cry in the shower of course. Because ugly crying in the shower is better. I was crying because didn’t know how to write. I had just signed up for an extremely expensive international studies. When I caught my breathe I decided to get out of the shower – I looked at myself in the mirror and laughed at myself.

“Get it together AnnMarie, go and figure out how to write”.

I went into the international office the next day and I said I needed extra time – I was still visibly in a fragile state. So they helped me extend my studies so I had a smaller course load each semester.

I ended up graduating
with honors

I hated writing so much that I had my mind set on getting SO much support with tutoring that the tutors would write my papers for me. HA! Joke was on me – they actually helped me learn how to write. I took every single extra tutoring course there was to improve my writing. The first 2 years it took me forever to write my papers.

After graduation, I interviewed at several companies just coming shy of getting the role with my degree. I was still in Australia and ended up getting a job with a company designing and selling custom kitchens. I loved it!

Fast forward, I came back home to Canada after I ran out of visas. I continued working in Cabinetry design until I got fired for being too persistent doing things my own way. Before I was fired, I started working with a business coach and came to her with nothing. I said “I don’t know what I want to do but I want to be successful and I want to help people”.

We threw around a few ideas and ADHD Coaching was born.

I went all in on my coaching business

My first group coaching program was successfully a 5 figure launch and successfully completely shifted and transformed the lives of many ADHDers.

I have a lifetime of managing my own ADHD through Junior High, High School, and two Post-Secondary Universities, a successful career, and successfully navigating business and entrepreneurship.

With a Bachelor of Health Promotion and as a student of Elite Coaching Programs from global top performance Coaches I incorporate Neuro Linguistic Programming techniques to help clients RELEASE old unproductive habits, and create their VISION with the power of their SUBCONSCIOUS MIND to help them completely transform their lives and their relationship with ADHD.

The foundation of my work is ADHD Transformation because without it – truly it is not possible to create the results you want in your business. You must first learn how to navigate your brain in a powerful way THEN can focus on getting the results in your business.

My Purpose!

I believe navigating ADHD can be EASY and graceful. Same with running a business. I am passionate about changing the narrative that living with ADHD doesn’t have to be difficult. It can be easy when you have the right mental tools to navigate your brain.

So through my free content and my coaching programs – I actively create change in this world to shift that narrative and to give people the right tools to navigate their lives and business.

Please reach out to connect with me and share your story with ADHD. Because the more we contribute as a collective to telling our stories and building networks, the more we can start to create a safe space for other people with ADHD to show up as their true authentic selves.

Sending you much love and success,

AnnMarie
